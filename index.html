What's going on right now?
I Am Not Flat!
The planet bulges around the equator by an extra 0.3 percent as a result of the fact that it rotates about its axis.
The Days Are Getting Longer
The length of Earth's day is increasing. Today, the average day is 24 hours long, but is increasing by about 1.7 milliseconds every century.
There Weren't Always Several Continents
About 250 million years ago, the continents came together once again to form another supercontinent called Pangaea, surrounded by a single, worldwide ocean. Fifty million years later, Pangaea began to break apart.
The Driest Place On Earth
The driest place in the world — the Atacama Desert in northern Chile — is next to the biggest body of water — the Pacific Ocean. Average annual rainfall in Arica, Chile, is just 0.8 millimeters (0.03 inches).
Our Sun Has A Voracious Appetite
All stars, like our sun, age and eventually die. As the sun exhausts its supply of hydrogen, it will collapse under gravity, ultimately ballooning into a red giant that is 100 times bigger and 2,000 times more luminous, vaporizing Earth in the process. But don't worry; it won't happen for about five billion years.
The Moon Is Not Earth's Only Companion
There are two other bodies orbiting near Earth that are sometimes referred to as moons--Cruithne and Asteroid 2002 AA29. They are both orbits the sun once a year but since it takes the same amount of time to orbit the sun as Earth, it looks as if they are following our planet.
